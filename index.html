Introduction

During the British colonial rule in India in 1892, under Government Order No. 1010/10-A, conditional (Panchami) lands were allotted to the Depressed Classes for their social and economic advancement.

These lands were legally reserved exclusively for the Depressed Classes and could not be sold or transferred to others.

This assignment and its conditions were recommended by J. H. A. Tremenheere, the then Collector of Chengalpattu, and were examined and brought into effect during the reign of Queen Victoria.

Executive Leadership of the Land Campaign

This Land Campaign is led by social activists with decades of grassroots experience.

Our main focus is to safeguard Panchami lands, prevent illegal encroachments, and restore them to their rightful owners.
