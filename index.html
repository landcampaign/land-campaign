Introduction

In 1892, under G.O. No. 1010/10-A, Depressed Classes Conditional (Panchami) lands were assigned for the social and economic advancement of the Depressed Classes.

These lands are legally reserved exclusively for the Depressed Classes and cannot be sold or transferred to others.

This assignment and its conditions were recommended by J. H. A. Tremenheere, the then Collector of Chengalpattu, and were examined and brought into effect through a Government Order by Queen Victoria. We express our sincere respect and gratitude to these visionary leaders for their historic contribution.

About the Land Campaign

This campaign is led by social activists with over three decades of grassroots experience.

Our mission is to safeguard Panchami (D.C.) lands, prevent unlawful encroachments, and ensure that conditional lands are restored to their rightful owners.
